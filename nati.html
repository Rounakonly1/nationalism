What is Nationalism?

Nationalism is the powerful feeling of love and loyalty towards one's country. It's the belief that people who share the same culture, language, and history should unite to form their own sovereign nation.

How Nationalism Began

The seeds of nationalism were planted during the 18th and 19th centuries in Europe, particularly during the transformative era of the French Revolution (1789). This period introduced radical ideas of liberty, equality, and brotherhood that reshaped people's understanding of nationhood.

People began to realize that political legitimacy should come from the nation's people rather than monarchs or foreign powers. This awakening spread across continents, inspiring independence movements worldwide.

The Indian Nationalist Movement

Nationalism in India emerged powerfully in the 19th century as a response to British colonial rule. The exploitation of India's resources and the imposition of unfair laws gradually united diverse communities under a common dream of independence.

Movements like the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920), Civil Disobedience Movement (1930), and Quit India Movement (1942) demonstrated the strength of Indian nationalism, ultimately leading to independence in 1947.
